"The Grapes of Wrath"

By John Steinbeck

The Grapes of Wrath is John Steinbeck's best known novel, following the Joads, a poor family
as they travel from their abandoned homestead in Oklahoma to southern California during the
dust-bowl years in the Great Depression (1929-1939). During their migration, the Joads face
many hardships, including deaths in the family and run-ins with the law.

Grapes of Wrath was heavily criticized and even banned in many places after first being published,
but over time the novel has become an American classic. While not often read in school, 'Wrath'
continues to attract wide readership across many demographics and has become an enduring staple
of Americna Literature.
